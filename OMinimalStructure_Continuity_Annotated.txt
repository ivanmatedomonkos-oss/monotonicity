# Line-by-line annotation of `OMinimalStructure_Continuity_Fixed.lean`

This is a companion explanation, not a replacement Lean file. Each non-empty original line is shown with its original line number, followed by an explanation of the Lean syntax and the mathematical role of that line.

## Conventions used throughout

* `Set A` in Mathlib is essentially `A -> Prop`: a set is represented by its membership predicate.
* `Prop` is the universe of propositions. A term of a proposition is a proof of it. This is the Curry-Howard viewpoint.
* `Type u` means a type living in universe level `u`. The line `universe u` declares this universe variable.
* Parentheses `(x : T)` usually introduce explicit arguments. Braces `{x : T}` introduce implicit arguments; Lean tries to infer them.
* `:=` separates the name/type of a declaration from its definition or proof.
* `:= by` starts tactic mode: after it, lines such as `simp`, `exact`, `rw`, `have`, and `rcases` build the required proof term.
* `fun x => ...` is lambda notation: it defines a function sending `x` to the expression after `=>`.
* `forall x : A, P x` is universal quantification. `exists x : A, P x` is existential quantification.
* `A /\ B` is conjunction, `A \/ B` is disjunction, `Not A` is negation, and `P -> Q` is implication.
* `{x : A // P x}` is a subtype: an element is a pair consisting of `x : A` and a proof of `P x`.
* `Fin n` is the type of natural numbers strictly less than `n`; it is used for coordinates of `R^n`.
